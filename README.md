# LeakLens

An independent, non-commercial project documenting data exposure incidents and providing a public tool to check whether your personal data appears in known leaks.

## About

Independent data protection oversight is inconsistent across jurisdictions — in many places, no authority actively audits how banks, fintech platforms, and government services handle the data they collect. LeakLens fills part of that gap: a public, documented registry of known leak incidents, plus a simple way for individuals to check their own exposure.

We are not a regulator, a law enforcement body, or a commercial security vendor. We document what has already been publicly disclosed, and we make it searchable.

## What We Do

- **Incident documentation.** Each entry in the registry is tied to a date, a source, and a link to the original disclosure or reporting.
- **Exposure lookup.** Check an email address or phone number against documented incidents.
- **Zero data retention.** We do not store search queries or the data submitted to check them. Nothing is logged after a check completes.

## What We Don't Do

- We do not collect, host, or redistribute raw leaked data.
- We do not perform threat actor attribution or original security research — we document publicly disclosed incidents, we don't investigate breaches ourselves.
- We are not affiliated with any government, financial institution, or commercial security company.

## Sources

Entries are built from public reporting by independent investigative organizations and cross-referenced against publicly disclosed incidents. Every entry links to its original source. We do not add an incident to the registry without a verifiable public source.

## Methodology

Our verification standards, inclusion criteria, and update process are documented on the official site. In short: an incident is added only after independent public confirmation, and each entry is reviewed before publication.

- **Official site:** [leaklens.org](https://leaklens.org)
- **Methodology & FAQ:** see the official site for full detail
- **Report an incident / contact:** secure channels listed on the official site

## Disclaimer

LeakLens is an independent civic project, not a government body, and not a substitute for legal or regulatory advice. If you believe your data has been exposed, we recommend also contacting the relevant service provider directly.
